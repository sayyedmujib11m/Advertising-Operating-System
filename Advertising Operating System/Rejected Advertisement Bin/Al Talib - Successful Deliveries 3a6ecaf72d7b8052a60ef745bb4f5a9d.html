Creative Reverse Engineering

Business Problem

Logistics companies offer similar services, making differentiation difficult.

Business Goal

Position Al Talib Shipping as a reliable logistics partner focused on successful deliveries rather than simply transporting goods.

Communication Objective

Convince customers that the company measures success by completed deliveries instead of distance traveled.

Communication Direction

Success is measured by delivery outcomes, not by kilometers driven.

Big Idea

A giant speedometer represents transportation performance while the copy reframes success as completed deliveries.

(This is where the concept begins to weaken because the visual still emphasizes speed rather than successful completion.)

Why It Is Rejected

This advertisement has an idea, but it doesn't develop that idea into a strong advertising concept.

The speedometer is simply enlarged behind the truck to communicate speed. This is a very direct visual association and doesn't create a memorable conceptual relationship between the product, the object, and the business promise.

The headline:

"We don't count kilometers. We count successful deliveries."

actually communicates reliability and delivery success, while the visual communicates speed. These two ideas are related but not identical, creating a slight disconnect between the copy and the visual.

What It Lacks

  • No surprising visual transformation.
  • Weak metaphorical depth.
  • The speedometer functions more as an oversized prop than as a storytelling device.
  • Product/service isn't integrated into the concept—it is merely placed in front of it.
  • The communication direction and execution are not fully aligned.

Research Value

Although rejected, it contributes a useful boundary for the Scale & Proportion Ads module.

Knowledge Learned

Increasing the size of an object does not automatically create a conceptual advertisement.

For Scale & Proportion to become a strong visual thinking system, the enlarged object must actively participate in communicating the idea—not simply reinforce an obvious keyword.

Compare:

  • Weak: Giant speedometer = fast delivery.
  • Strong: Giant compass becoming the navigation system because accurate direction is the service promise.

The latter example make the object part of the storytelling rather than a background illustration.
