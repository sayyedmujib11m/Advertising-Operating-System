Research Note #02

Subject

Is Split Visual really a Visual Thinking System?

Observation

After analysing multiple advertisements, a pattern has started to emerge.

The visual split itself does not communicate the idea.

Instead, the split is simply an execution method used to reveal two contrasting states, realities or environments.

Examples observed so far:

American Transplant Association

Two lives.

One body.

The split reveals both realities simultaneously.

EcoOrigin Air Conditioner

Extreme heat.

Indoor comfort.

The split reveals both environments simultaneously.

Hypothesis

Split Visual may not be a primary visual thinking system.

It appears to function as an execution technique used to communicate another underlying concept.

Evidence

Removing the visible split line from both advertisements would weaken the composition but would not destroy the central idea.

This suggests the split is supporting the communication rather than creating it.

Working Principle

The creative idea exists before the split.

The split is one possible method of expressing that idea.

Questions to Investigate

Can the same Big Idea be executed without using a split layout?

Are there advertisements where the split itself is the core communication device?

What underlying concepts most commonly use split execution?

Evidence collected: 2 advertisements

Conclusion: Not enough evidence to rename or remove the "Split Visual" category.

Execution techniques (split, diagonal division, overlay, masking, etc.) may eventually become a separate section focused on visual execution methods, while conceptual systems remain focused on how ideas are generated.

CRN 001 — Characterization / Anthropomorphism Ads

Observation

This advertisement does not rely on object transformation, visual metaphor, or product exaggeration as its primary communication device. Instead, it gives human identity, emotions, body language, and social roles to products.

The induction cooktop behaves like a victorious king, while the LPG cylinder behaves like a defeated opponent.

The advertisement communicates entirely through character interaction.

Initial Definition

Characterization / Anthropomorphism Ads are advertisements in which products or objects are assigned human characteristics—including facial expressions, emotions, gestures, posture, personalities, or social roles—to communicate the advertising message.

Unlike Product Transformation Ads, the product remains physically recognizable while its behavior becomes human.

Core Communication Principle

People naturally understand emotions faster than technical comparisons.

By assigning personalities to products, the advertisement transforms functional competition into an emotional story.

Consumers understand:

  • who wins
  • who loses
  • who is confident
  • who is weak

without reading specifications.

Characteristics Observed

  • Human facial expressions
  • Human body language
  • Human emotions
  • Social identity (king, hero, villain, loser)
  • Character interaction
  • Emotional storytelling
  • Product identity remains intact

Difference From Existing Modules

Product Transformation Ads

Product physically becomes another object.

Example:

Pencil becomes an airplane.

Visual Metaphor Ads

Product represents another concept.

Example:

Perfume shadow becomes a mosque.

Characterization / Anthropomorphism Ads

Product behaves like a human.

Example:

Induction cooktop becomes a victorious king.

Communication Advantages

  • Makes technical products emotionally engaging.
  • Simplifies competitive messaging.
  • Creates memorable brand personalities.
  • Improves recall through storytelling.
  • Allows products to "act" instead of simply being displayed.

Limitations

May reduce premium perception if overused.

Requires expressive illustration or CGI.

Works better for emotional positioning than factual product demonstrations.
